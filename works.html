SF City Clinic - Web Redesign

San Francisco City Clinic


How might we create...?
A digital transformation for an health clinic

What is San Francisco City Clinic?

San Francisco City Clinic’s mission is to improve our community’s sexual health by providing free or low-cost sexual health care. The goal was to redesign their website to provide a user-centric environment that provided their information in a structured and meaningful way to achieve a stronger, trusting, and healthier community for all individuals.

The Original Website: http://www.sfcityclinic.org/

The Problem

San Francisco City Clinic has a website that is outdated and doesn’t reflect their values and is difficult to navigate making their content inaccessible.

The Goal

We will give San Francisco City Clinic users immediate access to the services and resources they want by organizing the content effectively and giving multiple access points and paths to their goals.

My Role:

UX/UI Designer

Tools:

Adobe XD, Adobe Illustrator, Invision

Deliverables:

UI style guide, brand voice, IA, site map, wireframes, & responsive high-fidelity mockups

The Discovery Process

Researching the Website: Heuristic Analysis

The Evaluation

Websites should be usable, accessible, and mobile-device ready. Using a heuristics evaluation checklist, I found various usability problems, in which leads to negative consequences on interaction of users with the website.

Main Finidings

  • - The information architecture and the applied navigation were confusing and small.

  • - The navigation choices were not logical

  • - The main user flows (like About You or City Clinic Results) were not intuitive

  • - Critical paths were not clear

  • - City Clinic Results did not provide a navigation

  • - Link names and buttons did not match content

  • - The design of links were not consistent and there were many that were downloadable PDFs

  • - Accessibility is an issue - the contact information was only on the home page and there was not a consistent footer

Cardsorting

Key Findings

We conducted the card sorting to better understand how users look for information they need that makes sense. Our main finding was that people group things that are related by topic, and then by chronological order if relevant. They created subcategories for topics that seemed to fit with a category to the different areas that could still be explored within them. This was also categorized by hierarchy of importance.

Real People - Usability Testing

Test Tasks

To understand the online experience of San Francisco City Clinic’s website, we benchmarked the desktop user experience through a selection of tasks:

The following pages were tested:

  • - Homepage

  • - Our Services

  • - City Clinic Results

  • - STD Basics

The Results?

  • - Usability is critical to adoption and meaningful use
  • - Lack of usability in an individual can cause not only frustration and non-use. It can be harmful when medical errors occur because a user could not find or did not see relevant information.
  • - Both utility (usefulness, how well a system handles the work a user must do) and ease-of use are part of usability.
  • - However, perception is important; and systems also have to be convenient, pleasurable, and meaningful. People use systems that they like, that they feel comfortable with, that give them value without overloading them.

Rebranding: Defining New Structural & Visual Rules

Sitemap - Brand Voice - UI Style Guide

We wanted the design to establish the foundation of SFCC. Redefining the sitemap, brand voice, and style guide would help create and reinforce the mission, reflection, and value that SFCC thought they were expressing to their users.

Design with intent: Sitemap

With the card sorting categories, we were able to have an idea of how we are going to display the categories and contents for the navigation bar and for every page.

We were about to put it in sections for:

  • - Get Care

  • - Get Involved

  • - Resources

  • - About Us

This made sense to first time and returning users to find exactly what they need.

Brand Voice

We defined 6 adjectives that would influence style, perspective, personality, and mission. We wanted to ensure that the user is aware that SFCC is user-centric, professional, educational, friendly, and supportive.

Dependable

Driven by a need to feel a sense of belonging. The clinic is motivated to provide reassurance, service, advice, listening and an open heart to support the welfare of others.

Supportive

A clinic that is accommodating and is giving or ready to give help. We want genuinely to help. This is a clinic that possesses a charming sincerity.

Causal

Casual approach to life, preferring to be understated, non-threatening and helpful to all. Relatable. The clinic can be portrayed through an upbeat and positive tone of voice that is inviting, open and accessible.

Respectable

Being respectful on is one of the focus we provide users with. The clinic believes that everyone matters equally, regardless of status, age, ethnicity or creed. By constantly immersing ourselves in the worlds of our users, we ensure that our services and resources always address real-world needs of all people with different lifestyles.

Educational

A clinic that is knowledgeable and well informed. A description of a clinic that has well-researched resources. Reflects a thorough knowledge about male, female and non-binary needs.

The new SFCC's Style Guide

San Francisco City Clinic’s brand guideline was developed with one goal in mind: to create a shared visual brand identity that reflects being trustworthy, contextual, easy-to-use, and for the user.
